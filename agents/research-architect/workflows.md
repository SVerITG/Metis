# PhD Architect Workflows

## 1. Central thesis workflow

Inputs:

- central framework document
- seeded literature
- supervision meetings
- current paper statuses

Process:

1. Maintain one central anchor note.
2. Keep the working thesis title visible.
3. Keep the central research question explicit.
4. Record the logic linking the papers.
5. Record unresolved conceptual questions.

## 2. Article-map workflow

For each article:

1. Define its core research question.
2. Define what role it plays in the thesis.
3. Define which methods it depends on.
4. Define which literature anchors it.
5. Define what is still missing.

The article map should distinguish:

- active papers
- future papers
- speculative ideas

## 3. Coherence-check workflow

PhD Architect should continuously check:

- whether articles overlap too much
- whether there is a missing conceptual bridge
- whether the thesis question is too broad or too narrow
- whether methods support the claims being made
- whether the sequence of papers makes narrative sense

## 4. Future-paper workflow

For future paper ideas:

1. assess how strongly they support the thesis spine
2. classify them as:
   - essential
   - useful
   - optional
   - distracting
3. explain why

## 5. Gap-detection workflow

PhD Architect should identify:

- conceptual gaps
- evidence gaps
- methods gaps
- linkage gaps between papers
- policy-translation gaps

## 6. Meeting-integration workflow

When supervision or research meetings are added:

1. read the structured meeting output
2. update the article map if decisions affect paper direction
3. record unresolved issues in the central note
4. convert key decisions into research-structure updates

## 7. Framework-evolution workflow

The post-elimination framework note should be maintained as a living structure.

It should include:

- competing surveillance models
- assumptions
- dependencies on future evidence
- operational constraints
- cross-disease analogies
