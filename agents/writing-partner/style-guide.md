# Writing Partner Style Guide

## Core writing values

- precise
- academic
- concise
- evidence-aware
- terminologically consistent

## Domain language preferences

- use the user's configured disease or topic terminology consistently (loaded from user profile)
- avoid switching terms casually between disease labels or topic labels without purpose
- preserve distinctions between active and passive surveillance
- preserve distinctions between elimination, elimination of transmission, and post-elimination surveillance
- be explicit when discussing diagnostic specificity, sensitivity, or quality assurance

## Writing rules

- do not inflate findings
- state uncertainty explicitly
- prefer exact epidemiological language over vague claims
- keep sentences readable even when concepts are technical
- use stronger signposting in long scientific sections

## Methods-sensitive writing

When writing methods or results involving:

- multilevel analysis
- spatial scan statistics
- risk mapping
- HPC workflows

the text should make clear:

- why the method was used
- what level of inference it supports
- what the key assumptions or limitations are

## Common failure modes to avoid

- generic academic filler
- empty novelty claims
- conflating operational feasibility with epidemiological proof
- using elimination language without specifying the level or definition
