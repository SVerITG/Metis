## S–Z Terms (50+)

1. **Sampling frame** — list of units eligible for sampling. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
2. **Sampling variability** — variation between estimates from different random samples. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
3. **Screening** — testing asymptomatic people to detect early disease. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/10-screening-and-surveillance.md`.
4. **Secondary data** — using existing datasets for new analyses (registries, labs). Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
5. **Self-report** — data provided by participants, vulnerable to recall bias. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
6. **Sensor data** — digital signals (mobility, temperature) feeding modern surveillance. Links: `06_library/concepts/digital-health-epi.md`.
7. **Sensitivity** — probability of test correctly identifying disease when present (TP/(TP+FN)). Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
8. **Seroconversion** — development of detectable antibodies post-infection. Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
9. **Serial interval** — time between symptom onset of successive cases. Links: `06_library/courses/outbreak-investigation/lessons/04-descriptive-epidemiology-in-outbreaks.md`.
10. **Seroprevalence** — proportion with antibodies, measuring cumulative exposure. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
11. **Signal detection** — algorithms for identifying anomalies (spikes). Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
12. **Simpson’s paradox** — aggregate trends reverse after stratification. Links: `06_library/methods/causal-inference.md`.
13. **Skewness** — asymmetry of a probability distribution. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
14. **Smoothed rate** — stabilized estimate via spatial or temporal averaging. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/03-disease-mapping.md`.
15. **Social determinants** — conditions (income, housing) driving health inequities. Links: `06_library/concepts/health-equity-sdh.md`.
16. **Specificity** — probability test negative when disease absent (TN/(TN+FP)). Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
17. **Spatial autocorrelation** — clustering measured by Moran’s I. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/05-spatial-autocorrelation.md`.
18. **Spatial heterogeneity** — geographic variability requiring tailored interventions. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/06-spatial-regression.md`.
19. **Spatial lag** — neighbor effect included in regression modeling. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/06-spatial-regression.md`.
20. **Spatial scan statistic** — Kulldorff’s SaTScan approach for cluster detection. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/04-cluster-detection-satscan.md`.
21. **Spike** — sudden rise in cases triggering further investigation. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
22. **Stakeholder engagement** — involving communities/key actors for ethics and data stewardship. Links: `06_library/courses/research-ethics/lessons/05-justice-equity-and-community-engagement.md`.
23. **Standard deviation (SD)** — average distance from mean. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
24. **Standard error (SE)** — SD of sampling distribution (SD/√n). Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
25. **Standardized morbidity ratio (SMR)** — observed-to-expected morbidity ratio. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/03-disease-mapping.md`.
26. **Standardized mortality ratio** — as above but for mortality. Links: same as 25.
27. **Standardization** — adjustment to remove confounding (e.g., age). Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
28. **Stratified analysis** — evaluating effects within strata to control confounding. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
29. **Strategy map** — linking elimination phases to indicators and actions. Links: `06_library/courses/ntd-elimination/README.md`.
30. **Surveillance cycle** — plan → collect → analyze → interpret → disseminate. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
31. **Surveillance sensitivity** — ability to detect true cases; often expressed as proportion of cases captured. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
32. **Syndromic surveillance** — detecting trends via syndromes prior to lab confirmation. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
33. **Systematic error** — bias that consistently distorts estimates. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
34. **Systematic review** — structured, reproducible review of literature. Links: `06_library/courses/research-writing/lessons/10-systematic-reviews-prisma.md`.
35. **Survivorship bias** — ignoring those lost before measurement. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
36. **Survival analysis** — modeling time-to-event data. Links: `06_library/courses/biostatistics/lessons/10-survival-analysis.md`.
37. **Symptom-based surveillance** — using symptoms as proxies for disease detection. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
38. **Synthetic control** — counterfactual method combining untreated units. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
39. **System-level indicator** — measure reflecting entire health system function. Links: `06_library/concepts/health-systems-strengthening.md`.
40. **Target trial emulation** — observational design mirroring RCT for causal inference. Links: `06_library/reading-lists/essential-epidemiology-papers.md`.
41. **Temporal trend** — systematic change across time. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
42. **Tertiary data use** — analyzing previously published data for new questions. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
43. **Time to event** — duration from baseline to outcome; central to survival analysis. Links: `06_library/courses/biostatistics/lessons/10-survival-analysis.md`.
44. **Time series analysis** — analyzing data collected at regular time intervals. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
45. **Triage** — prioritizing cases/interventions based on severity/risk. Links: `06_library/methods/outbreak-investigation.md`.
46. **Underreporting** — incomplete capture of events due to system gaps. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
47. **Underascertainment** — missed cases because of low sensitivity or coverage. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
48. **Validity** — extent a measure reflects the intended concept. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
49. **Variance** — average squared deviation from mean. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
50. **Vector control** — interventions reducing vector populations to interrupt diseases. Links: `06_library/courses/ntd-elimination/lessons/03-intervention-packages-for-elimination.md`.
51. **Viral load** — pathogen quantity per unit sample used for diagnostics/surveillance. Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
52. **Vulnerable population** — groups with elevated risk or fewer resources. Links: `06_library/concepts/health-equity-sdh.md`.
53. **Wastewater surveillance** — monitoring sewage for pathogen DNA/RNA as an early warning. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
54. **Wave** — successive outbreak surges (e.g., COVID waves). Links: `06_library/concepts/current-challenges-2026.md`.
55. **Weighting** — adjusting values to reflect design or importance. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
56. **Wilcoxon test** — nonparametric test for ordinal data. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
57. **World Health Organization (WHO)** — global health authority shaping guidelines/certifications. Links: `06_library/concepts/global-health-governance.md`.
58. **Write-up** — formal documentation of methods/results per IMRaD. Links: `06_library/courses/research-writing/lessons/05-writing-introduction.md`.
59. **Zero cases** — absence of detected cases requiring evidence of detection capacity before declaring elimination. Links: `06_library/courses/ntd-elimination/lessons/04-surveillance-in-the-endgame.md`.
60. **Zero-inflated model** — count model dealing with excess zeros common in elimination datasets. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
61. **Zoom level** — spatial scale for analysis (grid, district, province). Links: `06_library/methods/spatial-epidemiology.md`.

## A–D Terms (50+)

1. **Attack rate** — proportion of an at-risk population that develops the condition during an outbreak. Formula: cases / population at risk. Links: `06_library/methods/outbreak-investigation.md`.
2. **Acute** — short-term onset disease presentation typically used in surveillance. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
3. **Adjusted rate** — rate standardized to a reference population to remove confounding by age/sex. Formula: age-standardized sum. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
4. **Agent** — biological or physical factor that causes disease. Links: `06_library/concepts/health-systems-strengthening.md`.
5. **Age standardization** — reweighting by age distribution so populations are comparable. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
6. **Administrative data** — routinely collected records (registries, claims) used for surveillance. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
7. **Analytic study** — designs (cohort, case-control, experimental) focused on testing hypotheses. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/04-study-designs-overview.md`.
8. **Antigen** — a molecule that triggers immune response, relevant in vaccine surveillance. Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
9. **Antimicrobial stewardship** — coordinating antibiotic use to reduce resistance; informs surveillance surveillance data integration. Links: `06_library/concepts/current-challenges-2026.md`.
10. **Attributable fraction** — proportion of incidence attributable to exposure. Formula: (RR−1)/RR. Links: `06_library/methods/causal-inference.md`.
11. **Attributable risk** — difference in incidence between exposed and unexposed. Formula: Ie − Iu. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
12. **Average incubation period** — mean time from exposure to symptom onset; key in outbreak curves. Links: `06_library/methods/outbreak-investigation.md`.
13. **Baseline risk** — risk in unexposed/reference group. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
14. **Bias (selection)** — distortion from non-random subject selection. Links: `06_library/methods/study-designs.md`.
15. **Bias (information)** — distortion from measurement error, misclassification, or recall issues. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
16. **Bias (confounding)** — mixing of effects when exposure and outcome share determinants. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/09-bias-confounding-effect-modification.md`.
17. **Big data** — large, rapidly generated datasets (EHR, mobile) used for surveillance/trials. Links: `06_library/concepts/digital-health-epi.md`.
18. **Bimodal distribution** — two peaks in histogram, suggests multiple subpopulations. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
19. **Body mass index (BMI)** — weight/height^2; standard risk factor metric. Formula: kg/m^2. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
20. **Case definition** — set of criteria used to determine who counts as a case. Links: `06_library/courses/outbreak-investigation/lessons/03-case-definitions-and-line-listing.md`.
21. **Case fatality ratio (CFR)** — proportion of cases that die. Formula: deaths/cases. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
22. **Case series** — descriptive report of cases, no comparison group. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/04-study-designs-overview.md`.
23. **Case-control study** — retrospective design comparing exposures in diseased vs controls. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/06-case-control-studies.md`.
24. **Causality** — framework for judging exposure-outcome relationships (temporality, consistency). Links: `06_library/methods/causal-inference.md`.
25. **Causal diagram (DAG)** — visual representation of assumed relationships between variables. Links: `06_library/methods/causal-inference.md`.
26. **CDC surveillance attributes** — usefulness, simplicity, flexibility, data quality, acceptability, sensitivity, PPV, representativeness, timeliness, stability. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
27. **Confidence interval (CI)** — range around estimate consistent with data. Formula: estimate ± z×SE. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
28. **Cluster** — grouping of cases in time/place. Links: `06_library/courses/spatial-epidemiology/lessons/03-disease-mapping.md`.
29. **Cohort study** — prospective/retrospective follow-up from exposure to outcome. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/05-cohort-studies.md`.
30. **Common-source outbreak** — exposures from a single point (food event). Links: `06_library/courses/outbreak-investigation/lessons/05-hypothesis-generation.md`.
31. **Conditional probability** — probability of event given another event. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
32. **Confounding variable** — extraneous factor related to exposure/outcome. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/09-bias-confounding-effect-modification.md`.
33. **Control group** — group not receiving exposure/intervention; baseline comparison. Links: `06_library/methods/study-designs.md`.
34. **Control chart** — statistical process control tool for monitoring cases over time. Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
35. **Count data** — discrete numbers used in Poisson regression and rates. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
36. **Crude rate** — not adjusted measure (events/population). Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
37. **Cross-sectional study** — snapshot assessing exposure/outcome simultaneously. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/07-cross-sectional-studies.md`.
38. **Data quality** — dimensions of data (accuracy, completeness, timeliness). Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
39. **Data governance** — rules for data stewardship, sharing, and privacy. Links: `06_library/courses/research-ethics/lessons/04-privacy-confidentiality-and-data-governance.md`.
40. **Data linkage** — combining datasets via identifiers. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
41. **Data provenance** — metadata on where data came from and transformations. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
42. **Deductive reasoning** — logic starting from axioms to new conclusions (used in protocols). Links: `06_library/methods/study-designs.md`.
43. **Denominator** — population base for rates. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
44. **Derived variable** — computed indicator (e.g., age group) created from raw data. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
45. **Descriptive epidemiology** — analysis of time-place-person patterns. Links: `06_library/courses/outbreak-investigation/lessons/04-descriptive-epidemiology-in-outbreaks.md`.
46. **Detection threshold** — cutoff for declaring outbreak/signal (alert threshold). Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
47. **Deterministic model** — no randomness; used for planning elimination strategies. Links: `06_library/courses/ntd-elimination/lessons/03-intervention-packages-for-elimination.md`.
48. **Diagnostic test accuracy** — relation between test results and true disease (sensitivity/specificity). Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
49. **Disability-adjusted life year (DALY)** — years lost due to disability/mortality combined. Formula: YLL + YLD. Links: `06_library/concepts/health-equity-sdh.md`.
50. **Distribution (probability)** — description of how data values spread (normal, Poisson). Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
51. **Double burden** — simultaneous presence of infectious and noncommunicable diseases. Links: `06_library/concepts/current-challenges-2026.md`.
52. **Duration of exposure** — time spent under risk influencing cohorts. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
53. **Dynamic population** — entries/exits change denominator over time (e.g., surveillance catchments). Links: `06_library/methods/surveillance-systems.md`.
54. **Dyad** — pair of individuals or events (used in transmission chains). Links: `06_library/methods/outbreak-investigation.md`.

## M–R Terms (50+)

1. **Magnitude** — overall size of an outbreak or burden measured in counts or rates. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
2. **Mantel-Haenszel estimator** — stratification method for pooled estimates across confounder categories. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
3. **Marginal model** — regression describing marginal expectations (e.g., GEE). Links: `06_library/courses/biostatistics/lessons/12-intro-multilevel-models.md`.
4. **Matching** — pairing individuals across groups to control confounding (frequency/matched). Links: `06_library/methods/study-designs.md`.
5. **Masking (blinding)** — hiding assignment status to reduce bias. Links: `06_library/courses/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/08-experimental-studies.md`.
6. **Mean difference** — contrast in means between groups; used for continuous outcomes. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
7. **Median** — middle value splitting ordered data. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
8. **Mediation** — intermediary mechanism linking exposure to outcome. Links: `06_library/methods/causal-inference.md`.
9. **Metapopulation** — connected population units relevant for elimination planning. Links: `06_library/courses/ntd-elimination/lessons/06-equity-geography-and-the-last-mile.md`.
10. **Mixed-methods research** — integrates qualitative and quantitative data. Links: `06_library/methods/mixed-methods-research.md`.
11. **Missing at random (MAR)** — missingness depends on observed data only. Links: `06_library/methods/data-management.md`.
12. **Mode** — most frequent data value. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
13. **Morbidity** — occurrence of disease states (incidence/prevalence). Links: `06_library/coursesc/epidemiology-foundations/lessons/02-measures-of-disease-frequency.md`.
14. **Mortality rate** — deaths/person-time or population. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
15. **Multicollinearity** — predictors highly correlated, affecting regression estimates. Links: `06_library/methods/biostatistics-essentials.md`.
16. **Multilevel model** — hierarchical model for clustered data (e.g., districts/patients). Links: `06_library/courses/biostatistics/lessons/12-intro-multilevel-models.md`.
17. **Narrative review** — unsystematic literature summary; contrasted with systematic. Links: `06_library/courses/research-writing/lessons/10-systematic-reviews-prisma.md`.
18. **Negative predictive value (NPV)** — probability of not having disease when test negative. Formula: TN/(TN+FN). Links: `06_library/methods/diagnostic-test-evaluation.md`.
