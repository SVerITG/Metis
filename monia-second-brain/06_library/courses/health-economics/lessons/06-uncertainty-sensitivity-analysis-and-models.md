# Uncertainty, Sensitivity Analysis, and Decision Models

## Learning objectives
- Describe major sources of uncertainty in economic evaluation.
- Explain why sensitivity analysis is necessary.
- Recognize the role and limits of decision models.

## Prerequisites
- Cost-effectiveness analysis.

## Content
Economic studies depend on uncertain parameters such as costs, effectiveness, adherence, disease progression, and discount rates. Sensitivity analysis explores how conclusions change when these assumptions vary. One-way sensitivity analysis changes one parameter at a time, while probabilistic approaches vary many parameters together.

Decision models are often needed when evidence must be combined across studies or when long-term outcomes are not directly observed. Models are useful, but only when their structure, assumptions, and data sources are transparent.

## Key takeaways
- Economic conclusions are only as credible as their assumptions.
- Sensitivity analysis shows how robust a decision is.
- Models are decision tools, not substitutes for evidence.

## Self-check questions
1. Why is sensitivity analysis essential?
2. Name one major source of uncertainty in economic studies.
3. What is a decision model used for?
4. Why must model assumptions be explicit?
5. What is one limitation of modeled long-term outcomes?

## Answer key
1. Because key inputs are uncertain and conclusions may change.
2. Cost inputs, effect estimates, adherence, or discount rates.
3. To combine evidence and project outcomes beyond observed data.
4. Because hidden assumptions can drive the result.
5. They depend on assumptions that may not hold in reality.
