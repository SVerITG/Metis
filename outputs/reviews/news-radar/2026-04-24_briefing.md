### 2. **Post-validation surveillance for NTDs — resurgence detected even after elimination validation**

**Source:** MDPI Global Health (2026 scoping review: *Global Lymphatic Filariasis Post-Validation Surveillance Activities in 2025*)  
**Domain:** Post-elimination surveillance methodology, validation criteria, resurgence risk  
**What happened:** A 2025-2026 global review synthesized evidence from 23 countries/territories validated as having eliminated lymphatic filariasis (LF). Key finding: even after meeting WHO elimination criteria, persistent transmission can continue (e.g., Pacific Islands, Sri Lanka).

**Why it matters:** This is the exact methodological challenge your PhD is tackling. LF post-validation surveillance strategies (targeted surveys, integration into standard surveys, health-facility screening, molecular mosquito monitoring) are translatable to HAT. The evidence that re-activation is possible after validation directly informs post-elimination surveillance design.
