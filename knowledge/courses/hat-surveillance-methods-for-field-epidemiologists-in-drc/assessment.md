# Assessment — HAT Surveillance Methods for Field Epidemiologists in DRC

Graded items per module plus a final capstone. Each item's difficulty stays at or below the lesson's Bloom ceiling. Answer in your own words; a marker checks against the lesson Summary and worked examples.

## Module 1 — gHAT and the DRC Landscape (ceiling: Understand)
1. Describe the gHAT transmission cycle in 3-4 sentences, naming the vector, the principal reservoir, and the two clinical stages. (Understand)
2. Explain why the DRC is the pivotal country for the 2030 EPHP target. (Understand)

## Module 2 — Passive Surveillance (ceiling: Apply)
3. Given a one-paragraph patient vignette, state whether the person meets the gHAT suspect-case definition and what the next diagnostic step is. (Apply)
4. A facility screened 150 suspects and confirmed 3 cases. Compute the positivity rate and give one plausible interpretation. (Apply)

## Module 3 — Active Case Finding (ceiling: Apply)
5. From a list of 6 villages (with last-screening date, distance to focus, recent cases), select and justify the 3 to screen first. (Apply)
6. A round screened 900 of 1,200 eligible and confirmed 2 cases. Compute coverage and yield per 1,000; state whether you would call the village low-risk. (Apply)

## Module 4 — Test Performance (ceiling: Analyze)
7. For a test with sensitivity 90% and specificity 98% at 0.2% prevalence in 10,000 people, build the 2x2 table and compute PPV. (Analyze)
8. Analyse why over-screening a near-elimination population "floods the system with false positives," referring to your PPV calculation. (Analyze)

## Module 5 — Data Flow (ceiling: Apply)
9. Registers show 8 cases, DHIS2 shows 6, the national line list shows 7. List, in order, the reconciliation checks you would run to find the true count and the leak. (Apply)
10. Apply the four data-quality dimensions to a chain where two facilities did not report this quarter — which dimension fails and what is the surveillance consequence? (Apply)

## Module 6 — Vector Surveillance (ceiling: Apply)
11. Design a one-paragraph entomological monitoring plan (sentinel sites, baseline, indicator, re-trapping schedule) for a riverine focus receiving tiny targets. (Apply)
12. Baseline FTD = 10; post-deployment FTD = 1.5. Compute the percentage reduction and name one check before crediting the targets. (Apply)

## Module 7 — Analysis in R (ceiling: Analyze)
13. Write tidyverse code to compute incidence per 10,000 for a supplied cases/population table and flag zones at or above the EPHP threshold. (Analyze)
14. A choropleth shows one dark (high-incidence) zone whose total case count is 2. Analyse whether this is an alarm or an artefact and state what you would check. (Analyze)

## Module 8 — Elimination Endgame (ceiling: Evaluate)
15. Evaluate two zone dossiers with identical incidence but different surveillance coverage and data quality; recommend which (if any) to validate, with justification. (Evaluate)

## Capstone (ceiling: Evaluate)
You are given a synthetic evidence package for a DRC health zone: three years of incidence data, screening-coverage history, a data-quality summary, entomological monitoring results, and a map of recent cases.
- **Evaluate** the package against WHO EPHP criteria.
- **Appraise** the threats to the claim (cryptic transmission, surveillance decay, residual clustering).
- **Judge** the reintroduction risk and recommend a specific post-validation surveillance posture.
Deliverable: a one-page reasoned recommendation. This integrates Modules 1-8 and is the course's terminal Evaluate-level task.

## Grading
- Apply items: correct method + correct arithmetic + a sensible interpretation sentence.
- Analyze items: correct decomposition and reasoning, not just a number.
- Evaluate items / capstone: a defensible judgement with evidence cited from the dossier and explicit handling of uncertainty (especially the contested cryptic-transmission question).
