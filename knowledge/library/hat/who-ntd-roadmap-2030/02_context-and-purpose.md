# Section 1 — Context and Purpose of the Road Map

## Purpose and scope

The road map for neglected tropical diseases 2021–2030 is WHO's second blueprint for preventing, controlling, and where feasible eliminating and eradicating neglected tropical diseases. It follows the first road map, *Accelerating work to overcome the global impact of neglected tropical diseases* (2012), which set targets to 2020 for 17 NTDs. The new road map covers an expanded portfolio of 20 NTDs and disease groups.

The aim is to facilitate alignment among Member States and other stakeholders and to accelerate progress towards the prevention, control, elimination and eradication of the 20 NTDs — and attaining the SDGs.

## What are NTDs?

NTDs are a medically diverse set of 20 diseases and disease groups that disproportionately affect people living in poverty, predominantly in tropical and subtropical areas. They impose a human, social and economic burden on more than **one billion people** in all countries, particularly in low-income countries and the most disadvantaged communities in middle-income countries.

- More than 200,000 people die each year from snakebite envenoming, rabies, and dengue alone
- Hundreds of millions are severely disabled, disfigured, or debilitated — resulting in social exclusion, stigmatization and discrimination
- NTDs cost developing communities billions of US dollars annually in direct health costs, loss of productivity, and reduced socioeconomic and educational attainment

**Human African trypanosomiasis example:** HAT in the Democratic Republic of the Congo costs affected households in a typical rural community more than 40% of their annual household income (Lutumba et al., 2007).

## NTDs as best buys in global public health

NTD interventions are among the best buys in global public health:

- An estimated net benefit of approximately **US$ 25 for every US$ 1 invested** in preventive chemotherapy
- Represents a 30% annualized rate of return
- Contributes significantly to universal health coverage and social protection for the least well-off

## Linkages with the SDGs

NTDs are formally recognized as targets for global action in **SDG target 3.3**, which calls to "end the epidemics of neglected tropical diseases" by 2030, as part of Goal 3 (Ensure healthy lives and well-being for all at all ages). Successful NTD interventions also contribute to:

- Goal 1 (No Poverty) — alleviating poverty
- Goal 2 (Zero Hunger)
- Goal 4 (Quality Education) — enabling children to pursue education
- Goal 5 and 10 (Equality) — promoting gender equality
- Goal 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) — WASH interventions help eliminate/control NTDs
- Goal 9 (Infrastructure) — enables medicine delivery to remote communities
- Goal 11 and 13 (Sustainable cities, climate action) — environmental management for vector control
- Goal 17 (Global Partnerships)

## NTDs and universal health coverage

Tackling NTDs supports universal health coverage — that all individuals and communities receive health services they need without suffering financial hardship (SDG target 3.8). NTD interventions reach some of the world's most remote communities, improving equitable access to health care. Treatment coverage can serve as an indicator of the extent of universal health coverage.

## Progress under the 2012 road map

Significant progress was made under the first road map (2012–2020), but many of the 2020 targets were not achieved. The new road map builds on lessons learned and emerging challenges.

Key shifts from the 2012 to 2021–2030 road map:
- From **disease-specific** to **integrated** approaches cutting across all 20 diseases
- From externally-driven to **country-owned** programmes
- More emphasis on development of **new tools** for prevention, diagnosis, and treatment
- Greater coordination with other programmes and sectors
