# Section 2 — 2030 Targets and Milestones

## Target categories

Disease-specific targets for 2030 are set for one of four categories:

| Category | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Eradication** | Permanent reduction to zero of worldwide incidence of a specific pathogen; no risk of reintroduction |
| **Elimination (interruption of transmission)** | Reduction to zero of the incidence of infection in a defined geographical area; continued action may be needed to prevent re-establishment |
| **Elimination as a public health problem** | Achievement of measurable targets set by WHO; continued action required to maintain targets and/or advance interruption of transmission |
| **Control** | Reduction of disease incidence, prevalence, morbidity and/or mortality to a locally acceptable level; continued interventions required |

## Overarching global targets for 2030

| Indicator | Target |
|---|---|
| Percentage reduction in people requiring interventions against NTDs (vs 2010 baseline) | **90%** |
| Percentage reduction in DALYs related to NTDs | **75%** |
| Number of countries having eliminated at least one NTD | **100** |
| Number of NTDs eradicated | **2** |

| Integrated approaches | Integrated treatment coverage index for preventive chemotherapy | **75%** |
| Integrated approaches | Countries with skin NTD integrated strategies adopted | **40** |
| Country ownership | Share of countries with NTDs in national health strategies/plans | **90%** |

| Country ownership | Share of countries collecting data disaggregated by gender | **90%** |
| Universal health coverage | Share of population at risk protected against catastrophic out-of-pocket health expenditure | **90%** |
