# WHO NTD Road Map 2021–2030 — Executive Summary

## Overarching vision

The road map for neglected tropical diseases 2021–2030 sets global targets and milestones to prevent, control, eliminate, and eradicate 20 diseases and disease groups. It also sets cross-cutting targets aligned with WHO's Thirteenth General Programme of Work 2019–2023 and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

The document was prepared through extensive global consultation: regional workshops with national NTD programme managers, more than 100 bilateral interviews with stakeholders, and over 300 responses from three rounds of online consultations.

## Three strategic pillars

The road map is built on three pillars:

- **Pillar 1 — Accelerate programmatic action:** Continue and intensify interventions where gaps remain, particularly diagnostics, monitoring and evaluation, access and logistics, and advocacy and funding.
- **Pillar 2 — Intensify cross-cutting approaches:** Integrate approaches across diseases, mainstream delivery into national health systems, and coordinate across sectors (WASH, vector control, One Health, education, disability).
- **Pillar 3 — Change operating models and culture to facilitate country ownership:** Shift from externally-driven to nationally-owned programmes, with domestic financing, national NTD plans, and high-level political commitment.

## Key overarching targets for 2030

- **90% reduction** in the number of people requiring interventions against NTDs (compared to 2010 baseline)
- **90% reduction** in disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) related to NTDs
- **100 countries** having eliminated at least one NTD
- **2 NTDs eradicated** (dracunculiasis and yaws)

## Cross-cutting targets for 2030

- 75% reduction in deaths from vector-borne NTDs (vs 2016)
- 100% access to at least basic water supply, sanitation, and hygiene in NTD-endemic areas
- 75% integrated treatment coverage index for preventive chemotherapy
- 90% of countries with NTDs integrated in national health strategies
- 90% of the population at risk protected against catastrophic out-of-pocket expenditure

## Integrating and mainstreaming approaches

The road map calls for more radical change in how approaches are integrated and mainstreamed into national health systems. Cross-cutting concepts are not new — they appear in existing NTD plans — but operationalization has been problematic. Strengthening national health systems will ensure:

- Delivery of interventions through existing infrastructure
- Improved sustainability and efficiency
- Equitable patient access to treatment, care and support

Close coordination across sectors — vector control, WASH, animal and environmental health, health education, education and disability — will maximize synergies.

## Country ownership and delivery

Countries are both the drivers and beneficiaries of progress towards the 2030 NTD targets. National and local governments must:

- Lead agenda-setting and realize objectives, with partly or fully domestic funding
- Integrate and prioritize NTD prevention and control in national health plans
- Dedicate a corresponding budget line item
- Foster multisectoral action at ministerial level

At least 1.74 billion people still require interventions against NTDs at the time of the road map's adoption.

## Review schedule

Progress reviews are planned for 2022, 2024, 2026, and 2029. A final evaluation report will be produced in 2031.
