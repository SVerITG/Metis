Editorial body — two columns

Metis is, above all, a workbench. It borrows the logic of the reading room and the card catalog, and renders it in pixels for a single reader. The morning begins with a briefing — a paragraph, not a dashboard — and the day continues as a conversation between Stan and the system, sotto voce, in the margins.

The voice is plain. Short declaratives. Proper em-dashes and italics. No celebration. No badges. Where other products reach for icons, Metis prefers UPPERCASE MONO LABELS — a quiet, typographic signal that we are in a working archive, not a feed.

The serif does the human work; the sans holds the chrome; the mono, the metadata. This is the whole trinity. Everything else is restraint.

Voice — do / don't

Do
"Three threads from yesterday are still warm."
Metis observes. It offers rather than prompts. Specific nouns, quiet tone.
Don't
"Would you like me to help you with your tasks?"
Assistant-speak. Unspecific. Frames the human as petitioner, which Metis is not.
Do
"That couldn't be saved. The connection dropped; nothing was lost."
Error prose: declarative, reassuring, next-step implied.
Don't
"Oops! Something went wrong. Please try again!"
Exclamation, cartoon tone, no information. Not Metis.
