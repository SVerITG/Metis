# Epidemiology Foundations

This course is a beginner-friendly introduction to epidemiologic reasoning for public health practice. It draws on CDC self-study epidemiology lessons, Gordis-style core concepts, and practical field examples relevant to disease control, screening, and outbreak work.

## Audience
- Early-career epidemiologists
- Public health analysts
- Researchers moving into population-health methods

## Structure
- 12 lessons
- Foundations first, then major observational and experimental designs
- Final modules on bias, screening, causation, and outbreak investigation

## Core resources
- CDC Principles of Epidemiology in Public Health Practice
- CDC field epidemiology and outbreak resources
- OpenWHO outbreak and surveillance learning materials
- Related Metis cards in `06_library/methods/` and `06_library/concepts/`
