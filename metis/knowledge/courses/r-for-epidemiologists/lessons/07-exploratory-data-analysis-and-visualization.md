# Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization

## Learning objectives
- Use visualization to inspect distributions, outliers, and group differences.
- Explain why exploratory plots are part of data-quality assessment.
- Recognize common plotting mistakes in public health work.

## Prerequisites
- Reshaping and joining data.

## Content
Exploratory data analysis helps analysts understand distributions, missingness, outliers, and possible coding issues before formal modeling begins. `ggplot2` supports histograms, boxplots, scatterplots, bar charts, and faceted comparisons in a consistent grammar.

The point of visualization is not decoration. It is to discover structure, problems, and questions. Good plots reduce clutter, label clearly, and emphasize interpretation rather than novelty.

## Key takeaways
- Visualization supports both quality control and analysis.
- `ggplot2` provides a consistent plotting grammar.
- Simple, interpretable plots are usually better than flashy ones.

## Self-check questions
1. Why is visualization useful before modeling?
2. What is one thing a histogram can reveal?
3. Why should plots avoid clutter?
4. What does faceting help with?
5. Why are exploratory plots not just presentation tools?

## Answer key
1. Because they reveal anomalies, distributions, and relationships early.
2. Skew, unusual spikes, or impossible values.
3. Because clutter makes patterns harder to interpret.
4. Comparing patterns across subgroups.
5. They support data understanding and error detection.
