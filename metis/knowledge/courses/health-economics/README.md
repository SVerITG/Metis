# Health Economics for Public Health

This course introduces the core ideas of health economics that public health researchers and epidemiologists encounter when moving from burden estimation to decisions about resource allocation. It covers costs, consequences, economic evaluation, uncertainty, and the practical limits of translating economic evidence into policy.

## Audience
- Epidemiologists entering programme evaluation or policy work
- Public health researchers reading cost-effectiveness studies
- Programme staff involved in prioritization and scale-up decisions

## Structure
- 8 lessons
- Starts with economic reasoning and cost concepts
- Moves through cost-effectiveness and decision analysis
- Ends with equity, implementation, and policy interpretation

## Core resources
- WHO cost-effectiveness and UHC guidance
- iDSI reference case and CHEERS reporting guidance
- Open access teaching materials on economic evaluation
- Related Metis cards in `06_library/methods/` and `06_library/concepts/`
