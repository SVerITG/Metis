# Why Research Ethics Matters

## Learning objectives
- Explain why ethics is part of research quality rather than a bureaucratic add-on.
- Describe core ethical principles relevant to public health research.
- Recognize how ethical failures damage both participants and scientific credibility.

## Prerequisites
- Basic research methods.

## Content
Research ethics asks whether a study respects people, distributes burdens and benefits fairly, and manages risk responsibly. In public health and epidemiology, ethics is inseparable from quality. A badly designed study can be unethical because it exposes participants or communities to burden without producing trustworthy knowledge.

Core principles include respect for persons, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice, and accountability. These principles shape consent, confidentiality, sampling, data sharing, community engagement, and reporting. Ethics therefore enters decisions across the entire study life cycle.

## Key takeaways
- Ethics and scientific quality are tightly linked.
- Respect, justice, and risk management apply throughout the study life cycle.
- Ethical failure damages trust, participation, and usefulness of findings.

## Self-check questions
1. Why can a poorly designed study be ethically problematic?
2. Name three core ethical principles.
3. Why is ethics not only about consent forms?
4. How can ethical failure affect later research?
5. What is one way ethics influences study design?

## Answer key
1. Because participants may bear burden without generating valid knowledge.
2. Respect for persons, beneficence, justice, or non-maleficence.
3. Because it also covers risk, privacy, fairness, governance, and reporting.
4. It can reduce trust and willingness to participate in future work.
5. Through fair sampling, risk minimization, or data protection choices.
