# Research Ethics in Global Health

This course introduces practical research ethics for epidemiologists and public health researchers working across clinical, field, programme, and global health settings. It covers core principles, consent, community engagement, data governance, ethics review, and the ethical tensions that arise in emergencies and cross-border collaborations.

## Audience
- Epidemiologists conducting field and operational research
- Public health researchers preparing protocols and publications
- Programme staff navigating ethics review and data governance

## Structure
- 8 lessons
- Starts with principles and ethics review
- Moves through consent, data, and justice questions
- Ends with emergency ethics and publication integrity

## Core resources
- WMA Declaration of Helsinki
- CIOMS International Ethical Guidelines
- WHO ethics and emergency research guidance
- Related Metis cards in `knowledge/library/methods/` and `knowledge/library/concepts/`
