# Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTD) Overview

> Reference card — WHO NTDs, Roadmap 2030 targets, cross-cutting interventions, key organizations.

---

## WHO's 20 NTDs (+ additional conditions)

### Preventive Chemotherapy (PCT) Diseases
| Disease | Pathogen/Cause | Main Intervention |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Lymphatic filariasis** | Wuchereria bancrofti, Brugia spp. | MDA (ivermectin + DEC or albendazole); morbidity management |
| **Onchocerciasis** | Onchocerca volvulus | MDA (ivermectin); vector control |
| **Schistosomiasis** | Schistosoma spp. | MDA (praziquantel); WASH; snail control |
| **Soil-transmitted helminthiases** | Ascaris, Trichuris, hookworm | MDA (albendazole or mebendazole); WASH |
| **Trachoma** | Chlamydia trachomatis | SAFE strategy (Surgery, Antibiotics, Facial cleanliness, Environmental improvement) |

### Case Management Diseases
| Disease | Pathogen/Cause | Key Features |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Human African trypanosomiasis** | T.b. gambiense, T.b. rhodesiense | Active/passive surveillance; fexinidazole, acoziborole |
| **Chagas disease** | Trypanosoma cruzi | Vector control; screening blood supply; benznidazole |
| **Visceral leishmaniasis** | Leishmania donovani/infantum | Diagnosis + treatment; vector control; elimination target in South-East Asia |
| **Cutaneous/mucocutaneous leishmaniasis** | Leishmania spp. | Case management; various treatments |
| **Buruli ulcer** | Mycobacterium ulcerans | Early detection; antibiotics (rifampicin + clarithromycin); wound care |
| **Leprosy** | Mycobacterium leprae | MDT (dapsone + rifampicin +/- clofazimine); disability prevention |
| **Yaws** | Treponema pallidum pertenue | Azithromycin MDA (Morges strategy); target eradication |
| **Mycetoma** | Fungi or bacteria | Surgery + antifungals/antibiotics |
| **Chromoblastomycosis and deep mycoses** | Various fungi | Antifungals; surgery |

### Vector-Borne/Zoonotic/Other
| Disease | Pathogen/Cause | Key Features |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Dengue and chikungunya** | Flavivirus / Alphavirus | Vector control (Aedes); clinical management; dengue vaccine (Qdenga) |
| **Rabies** | Lyssavirus | Dog vaccination; PEP; target: zero human deaths from dog-mediated rabies by 2030 |
| **Taeniasis/cysticercosis** | Taenia solium | Sanitation; pig management; treatment |
| **Dracunculiasis (guinea worm)** | Dracunculus medinensis | Water filtration; containment; target eradication |
| **Echinococcosis** | Echinococcus spp. | Dog deworming; meat inspection; surgery/PAIR |
| **Foodborne trematodiases** | Clonorchis, Opisthorchis, Fasciola, Paragonimus | Treatment (praziquantel/triclabendazole); food safety |
| **Scabies and ectoparasites** | Sarcoptes scabiei | MDA (ivermectin); case management |
| **Snakebite envenoming** | Various venomous snakes | Antivenom access; prevention; first aid |
| **Noma** | Polymicrobial (associated with malnutrition) | Added to NTD list 2023; nutrition, oral hygiene, early treatment |

## NTD Roadmap 2030

### Vision
"A world free of neglected tropical diseases" — aligned with SDGs and UHC

### Overarching Targets
- 90% reduction in people requiring NTD interventions
- 75% reduction in DALYs due to NTDs
- Elimination in at least 100 countries
- Eradication of dracunculiasis and yaws

### Disease-Specific Targets (Selected)

| Disease | 2030 Target |
|---------|------------|
| Dracunculiasis | Eradication |
| Yaws | Eradication |
| gHAT | Elimination of transmission |
| Rabies (dog-mediated) | Zero human deaths |
| Leprosy | Elimination as public health problem (all countries) |
| VL (South-East Asia) | Elimination as public health problem |
| Trachoma | Elimination as public health problem |
| LF | Elimination as public health problem |
| Onchocerciasis | Elimination of transmission (selected countries) |
| Schistosomiasis | Elimination as public health problem |
| Chagas disease | Interruption of domiciliary vector-borne transmission |

### Three Strategic Pillars
1. **Accelerate programmatic action** — scale up proven interventions
2. **Intensify cross-cutting approaches** — WASH, vector control, One Health, veterinary public health
3. **Change operating models** — country ownership, integration, sustainability, mainstreaming

## Cross-Cutting Interventions

### Mass Drug Administration (MDA)
- **Principle:** Treat entire at-risk populations without individual diagnosis
- **Drugs donated:** Ivermectin (Merck), albendazole (GSK), azithromycin (Pfizer), praziquantel (Merck KGaA), others
- **Platforms:** School-based, community-based (community drug distributors)
- **Challenges:** Coverage gaps, systematic non-compliance, drug resistance risk, COVID-19 disruptions

### Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH)
- Critical for: STH, schistosomiasis, trachoma, guinea worm
- NTD-WASH nexus: integrated programming improves sustainability
- Challenge: WASH infrastructure investment far exceeds NTD programme budgets

### Vector Control
- **IRS, ITNs:** Malaria (and some NTDs)
- **Tiny targets:** Tsetse control for HAT
- **Larviciding, environmental management:** Dengue (Aedes)
- **Wolbachia:** Novel biological control for dengue (World Mosquito Program)
- **Gene drive:** Experimental; ethical debate ongoing

### Veterinary Public Health
- Dog vaccination for rabies
- Pig management for taeniasis/cysticercosis
- Livestock trypanocide use for rHAT foci
- Dog deworming for echinococcosis

### Integrated NTD Platforms
- Co-delivery of MDA for multiple NTDs
- Integration into primary healthcare
- Combined surveillance and mapping
- Shared supply chains and logistics

## Current Developments (2025-2026)

- **WHO's latest global synthesis is now the 2025 report:** Published on **22 October 2025**, the report summarizes progress made in **2023-2024** toward the NTD Roadmap 2030 and highlights diagnostics, monitoring and evaluation, access/logistics, and advocacy/funding as major cross-cutting themes.
- **Elimination milestones continued in early 2026:** WHO validated **Libya for trachoma elimination on 18 February 2026** and verified **Chile for elimination of leprosy on 4 March 2026**, both useful reminders that post-elimination surveillance and system retention matter.
- **Snakebite remains a live agenda item:** WHO released **new guidance on novel snakebite treatments on 27 February 2026**, showing that the NTD portfolio continues to evolve beyond the better-known preventive chemotherapy diseases.
- **Financing pressure is real:** The 2025 global report explicitly discusses NTD progress in a constrained and evolving funding environment, which is important for planning surveillance and integration strategies.

---

## Practical Examples

- **Rabies in Cambodia:** WHO's 2025 global report uses Cambodia as a practical One Health example of how human health, animal health, and surveillance can be aligned around elimination.
- **gHAT elimination efforts:** HAT remains one of the clearest examples of how diagnostics, surveillance, treatment innovation, and vector control need to be coordinated across the elimination pathway.
- **Integrated skin-NTD programmes:** Leprosy, yaws, Buruli ulcer, and other skin conditions continue to drive integrated case-finding and training models rather than fully vertical programmes.
- **Wolbachia for dengue control:** Biological vector-control programmes illustrate how NTD and broader arboviral control increasingly rely on ecosystem-aware interventions rather than treatment alone.
