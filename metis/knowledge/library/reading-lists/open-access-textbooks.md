# Open-Access Textbooks and Courses

> Free, accessible resources for building epidemiology, statistics, surveillance, spatial, and research-writing skills inside Metis.

---

## Top 5 Picks

1. **CDC Principles of Epidemiology (SS1978)** – Deep self-study text covering surveillance, outbreak investigation, and foundational biostatistics, ideal for initial orientation.
2. **OpenIntro Statistics** – A clear, free introductory text with exercises, simulation labs, and reproducible R examples that support the Biostatistics course.
3. **R for Data Science** – The tidyverse-based workflow book that unlocks data cleaning, visualization, and reproducible pipelines in R.
4. **The Epidemiologist R Handbook** – Field-oriented R recipes that align perfectly with epidemiologic surveillance workflows.
5. **EQUATOR Network Reporting Guidelines** – An essential portal to STROBE, CONSORT, PRISMA, and many other transparency standards.

## Annotated bibliography

### Epidemiology and surveillance

1. **CDC Principles of Epidemiology (SS1978).** https://stacks.cdc.gov/view/cdc/13178  
   Why it matters: Covers core epidemiologic principles, surveillance, outbreak investigation, and basic biostatistics in a self-paced format.

2. **CDC SS1978 course page.** https://archive.cdc.gov/www_cdc_gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/index.html  
   Why it matters: Breaks the lessons into browsable sections and interactive artifacts.

3. **WHO OpenWHO.** https://openwho.org/about  
   Why it matters: WHO’s MOOC platform has courses on outbreak response, surveillance, diagnostics, and operations.

4. **WHO EWARS.** https://www.who.int/emergencies/surveillance/early-warning-alert-and-response-system-ewars/  
   Why it matters: Practical training material for emergency- and outbreak-focused surveillance.

5. **WHO Public Health Intelligence Competency Framework.** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240115408  
   Why it matters: Competency architecture for modern surveillance and intelligence roles.

6. **WHO PHI Curriculum.** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/public-health-intelligence-curriculum  
   Why it matters: Turns the PHI framework into explicit learning modules.

7. **WHO Vaccination Coverage Cluster Surveys manual.** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/WHO-IVB-18.09  
   Why it matters: Gold-standard field sampling guidance for coverage surveys.

### Biostatistics and methods

8. **OpenIntro Statistics.** https://www.openintro.org/book/os/  
   Why it matters: Free, hands-on introduction to distributions, inference, and regression with exercises and R code.

9. **OpenIntro Introduction to Modern Statistics.** https://www.openintro.org/book/ims  
   Why it matters: Bridges classical inference with simulation, modern regression, and applied analysis.

10. **Hernán & Robins, What If.** https://miguelhernan.org/whatifbook  
    Why it matters: The advanced causal inference text that is freely available online.

11. **Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews.** https://training.cochrane.org/handbook/current  
    Why it matters: Authoritative guidance for evidence synthesis, data extraction, and critical appraisal.

12. **GRADE Handbook.** https://training.cochrane.org/resource/grade-handbook  
    Why it matters: Moves from evidence to certainty assessments and policy implications.

13. **NIH OBSSR Mixed Methods resources.** https://obssr.od.nih.gov/research-resources/mixed-methods-research  
    Why it matters: Entry point for integrating qualitative and quantitative public-health research.

### R, data, and spatial workflows

14. **The Epidemiologist R Handbook.** https://www.epirhandbook.com/  
    Why it matters: Applied R recipes for surveillance, outbreak analysis, and data cleaning.

15. **Geocomputation with R.** https://r.geocompx.org/  
    Why it matters: Step-by-step spatial data handling and mapping with R and tidyverse tools.

16. **R for Data Science (2nd ed).** https://r4ds.hadley.nz/  
    Why it matters: Core tidyverse workflow for cleaning, transforming, visualizing, and modeling data.

17. **Regression Modeling Strategies (Harrell).** https://hbiostat.org/doc/rms/book/  
    Why it matters: Advanced modeling guidance once regression basics are comfortable.

### Writing and reporting

18. **EQUATOR Network Reporting Guidelines.** https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/  
    Why it matters: Central hub for STROBE, CONSORT, PRISMA, STARD, TRIPOD, and many other guidelines.

19. **STROBE Statement.** https://www.strobe-statement.org/  
    Why it matters: The checklist every observational epidemiologist should consult early in study design and writing.

20. **PRISMA Statement.** https://www.prisma-statement.org/  
    Why it matters: Essential for transparent systematic reviews and meta-analyses.

## 2024-2026 highlights

1. **WHO Public Health Intelligence Framework (2025).** Reinforces PHI competencies, digital surveillance interoperability, and data stewardship at national levels. https://www.who.int/teams/emergency-intelligence/public-health-intelligence  
2. **WHO Global Health Observatory dashboard refresh (2024).** Provides new interactive modules for health equity and surveillance indicators. https://www.who.int/data/gho  
3. **OpenWHO 2024 dengue and cholera response series.** New modules covering modelling, data systems, and field investigation strategies. https://openwho.org/  
4. **R for Data Science (2nd ed) updates (2023–2024).** Adds new tidyverse workflows for data storytelling and reproducible reporting. https://r4ds.hadley.nz/  
5. **Lancet Public Health special issue (2024) on rebuilding trust and equity after COVID-19.** https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpub/issue/current

## Using this list inside Metis

1. Begin with the CDC SS1978 lessons, OpenIntro Statistics, and The Epidemiologist R Handbook.
2. Layer in What If, Cochrane Handbook, and Geocomputation with R for deeper analysis.
3. Consult OpenWHO, PHI materials, STROBE, PRISMA, and EQUATOR while briefing or writing papers.
