# Key Institutions in Global Health and Epidemiology

> Reference card — major organizations, research institutions, and journals.

---

## International Organizations

### World Health Organization (WHO)
- **HQ:** Geneva, Switzerland
- **Structure:** HQ + 6 regional offices (AFRO, AMRO/PAHO, SEARO, EURO, EMRO, WPRO) + 150+ country offices
- **Key departments:** Health Emergencies (WHE), NTDs, Communicable Diseases, UHC
- **Relevance:** Normative guidance, IHR, disease surveillance coordination, NTD Roadmap, medicine donation programmes
- **Key publications:** Weekly Epidemiological Record (WER), World Health Statistics, Global Health Observatory
- **WHO AFRO:** Brazzaville; IDSR framework; ESPEN (NTDs); African CDC collaboration

### Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
- **Location:** Atlanta, Georgia, USA
- **Key roles:** Disease surveillance, outbreak investigation, FETP support globally, lab reference
- **Global Health Security:** CDC presence in 60+ countries
- **Relevance:** Surveillance evaluation guidelines, EIS/FETP, lab methods, MMWR

### European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC)
- **Location:** Stockholm, Sweden (Solna)
- **Role:** EU agency for infectious disease surveillance and response
- **Key outputs:** Surveillance Atlas, Rapid Risk Assessments, TESSy (European Surveillance System)
- **EPIET/EUPHEM:** European field epidemiology training programmes

### Africa CDC
- **Location:** Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
- **Role:** African Union public health agency; continental disease intelligence, coordination
- **Programmes:** Regional Integrated Surveillance and Laboratory Network (RISLNET); FETP support
- **Established:** 2017

## Research and Academic Institutions

### Institute of Tropical Medicine (ITM) Antwerp
- **Location:** Antwerp, Belgium
- **Strengths:** Tropical medicine, HAT research and policy, diagnostics, global health training
- **Relevance:** Leading institution for HAT; close collaboration with DRC, other endemic countries
- **Key units:** Department of Biomedical Sciences, Department of Public Health, Department of Clinical Sciences
- **Programmes:** Master in Public Health, short courses, PhD supervision

### London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine (LSHTM)
- **Location:** London, UK
- **Strengths:** Epidemiology, biostatistics, public health, tropical medicine, global health
- **Key centres:** Centre for Mathematical Modelling, Malaria Centre, NTD COUNTDOWN
- **Relevance:** Major research output in infectious disease epi, clinical trials, health systems

### Swiss Tropical and Public Health Institute (Swiss TPH)
- **Location:** Basel, Switzerland (affiliated with University of Basel)
- **Strengths:** Malaria, NTDs, health systems, diagnostics, clinical trials in LMICs
- **Relevance:** Drug development, field research in sub-Saharan Africa

### Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health
- **Location:** Baltimore, Maryland, USA
- **Strengths:** Largest school of public health; epidemiology, biostatistics, global health
- **Key outputs:** Training in epi methods; research across infectious and non-communicable diseases

### Institut de Recherche pour le Developpement (IRD)
- **Location:** Marseille, France; offices worldwide
- **Strengths:** HAT modelling, vector ecology, tropical disease research
- **Relevance:** Long history of HAT research, particularly in Central Africa

### Other Notable Institutions
- **Karolinska Institutet** (Stockholm): Global health, infectious diseases
- **Erasmus MC** (Rotterdam): Infectious disease modelling, virology
- **Bernhard Nocht Institute** (Hamburg): Tropical medicine
- **Nagasaki University** (NEKKEN): Tropical medicine, NTD research in Asia/Africa
- **Ifakara Health Institute** (Tanzania): Malaria, NTDs, health systems research

## Drug Development and Diagnostics Organizations

### DNDi (Drugs for Neglected Diseases initiative)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland
- **Mission:** Develop safe, effective, affordable treatments for neglected diseases
- **Key achievements:** ASAQ (malaria), NECT, fexinidazole, acoziborole (HAT); SSG&PM (VL)
- **Current pipeline:** HAT, VL, Chagas, mycetoma, dengue, hepatitis C, paediatric HIV

### FIND (Foundation for Innovative New Diagnostics)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland
- **Mission:** Develop and deliver diagnostics where they are needed most
- **Key areas:** TB, malaria, NTDs (HAT, VL), AMR, fever/febrile illness, pandemic preparedness

### Medicines for Malaria Venture (MMV)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland
- **Mission:** Discover, develop, deliver antimalarials
- **Key outputs:** Numerous antimalarials in the pipeline

### GARDP (Global Antibiotic Research & Development Partnership)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland
- **Mission:** Develop and deliver new antibacterial treatments
- **Focus:** Paediatric antibiotics, drug-resistant infections

## Humanitarian and Implementation Organizations

### Medecins Sans Frontieres (MSF)
- **Role:** Medical humanitarian aid in conflict, epidemics, exclusion from healthcare
- **Epi relevance:** Epicentre (MSF research arm); outbreak response; neglected disease treatment; operational research
- **HAT:** Historic role in HAT treatment and active screening, especially in DRC

### Partners in Health (PIH)
- **Focus:** Health system strengthening in poorest areas
- **Approach:** Accompaniment model; community health workers; social medicine
- **Presence:** Haiti, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Lesotho, others

## Current Developments (2025-2026)

- **WHO financing architecture changed materially:** WHO's Investment Round for **2025-2028** moved from concept to implementation, with the organization reporting at least **US$ 170 million** raised at the World Health Assembly pledging event on **20 May 2025** and more than **US$ 1.7 billion** in pledges reported in the 2024-2025 results overview.
- **ECDC updated its surveillance direction:** The **12 December 2025** mid-term revision of ECDC's long-term surveillance framework added pilots for novel data sources and methods, including electronic health records and large-scale data.
- **Africa CDC strengthened its operational infrastructure:** In **July 2025**, Africa CDC advanced a continental framework for decentralized diagnostics, and on **27 January 2026** it launched a Central Data Repository to integrate surveillance, laboratory, and programme data across the continent.
- **Practical implication:** The most influential institutions are currently investing in interoperability, flexible financing, and data integration, not just publishing guidance documents.

---

## Practical Examples

- **WHO as norm setter and fund mobilizer:** The Investment Round is a concrete example of how institutional financing affects what technical guidance and country support are possible.
- **Africa CDC and mpox readiness:** PAMTA and the decentralized diagnostics work show how an institution can translate epidemic lessons into laboratory and sequencing infrastructure.
- **ECDC surveillance pilots:** The 2025 framework revision illustrates how institutions are testing newer data streams while still preserving formal surveillance standards.
- **ITM, LSHTM, and Swiss TPH in practice:** These academic institutions remain important because they connect methodological training, field partnerships, diagnostics, and implementation research rather than operating only as teaching centres.
