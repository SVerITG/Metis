## Key terminology to use correctly

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|--------------|
| gambiense HAT | Always spell out on first use; abbreviate g-HAT if needed |
| post-elimination | After the WHO 2030 elimination target; not "eradication" |
| passive case detection | Diagnosing cases who seek care voluntarily (vs. active — mass screening) |
| health zone | DRC administrative unit for surveillance; prefer over "district" |
| SaTScan cluster | A statistically significant space-time cluster; not "hotspot" unless operationally defined |
| residual transmission | Ongoing transmission in low-endemicity settings; distinguish from "ongoing epidemic" |
| surveillance sensitivity | Proportion of true cases detected; always define the denominator |

1. **Introduction**: HAT elimination context → surveillance challenge in post-elimination phase → gap = where to focus passive surveillance
2. **Methods**: Study area (DRC/KC), data sources, SaTScan parameters, linkage to passive case counts
3. **Results**: Cluster map, temporal trends, spatial overlap with passive detection
4. **Discussion**: What do clusters tell us about passive detection gaps? Policy implications for surveillance

## Writing rules for this article

- Use **passive voice** sparingly — prefer active construction for methods
- Avoid "prove" and "demonstrate" — use "suggest", "indicate", "is consistent with"
- Quantify everything: cluster sizes (cases, population), p-values, relative risk
- Always qualify: "in the study area", "during the study period", "in the Kwilu-Congo province"
- Distinguish: "statistically significant" (SaTScan p<0.05) ≠ "programmatically significant"
